# Premium Medical Check Timeline Data

Use for executive health screening, premium medical check packages, concierge preventive checkups, and medical travel checkup itineraries. The goal is evidence-based prevention and appropriate follow-up, not maximum testing.

## Trusted Sources

- USPSTF recommendations: https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/
- USPSTF hypertension screening: https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/hypertension-in-adults-screening
- USPSTF prediabetes/type 2 diabetes screening: https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/screening-for-prediabetes-and-type-2-diabetes
- CDC adult immunization schedule: https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/hcp/imz-schedules/adult.html
- American Cancer Society cancer screening guidelines: https://www.cancer.org/cancer/screening/american-cancer-society-guidelines-for-the-early-detection-of-cancer.html
- Local national screening guidelines for the destination or patient's home country when specified.
- Treating physician's individualized plan overrides generic preventive schedules.

## Required Intake Details

Collect or state assumptions for:

- Age, sex at birth, gender-relevant anatomy such as cervix/prostate/breast tissue, pregnancy status.
- Symptoms: if acute or concerning symptoms exist, route to diagnostic care, not routine screening.
- Medical history: hypertension, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, cancer, kidney/liver disease, mental health, sleep apnea, infectious risks.
- Family history: early heart disease, breast/ovarian/colon/prostate cancer, inherited syndromes.
- Lifestyle and occupational risks: smoking pack-years, alcohol, exercise, diet, sexual health risks, travel exposure, hazardous work.
- Medications, allergies, vaccines, prior surgeries, prior screening dates/results.
- Goals: general prevention, second opinion, employer executive check, fertility, longevity, cancer anxiety, travel clearance.
- Follow-up access: home primary care doctor, specialist network, language, insurance, abnormal-result plan.

## Evidence-Based Package Structure

Include these modules when appropriate:

- Intake and physician exam: vitals, blood pressure, BMI/waist, medication review, family history, risk scoring.
- Baseline labs: selected by age/risk and local standard; avoid unsourced broad panels.
- Cardiometabolic risk: blood pressure, diabetes screening when indicated, lipid/CVD risk assessment when indicated.
- Cancer screening: breast, cervical, colorectal, lung, prostate shared decision-making, and other tests only if risk-based.
- Vaccines: review against current adult immunization schedule.
- Dental, vision, hearing, skin, bone health, sleep, mental health: include when age/risk/symptoms justify.
- Results conference: abnormal-result triage, written plan, referrals, home-country follow-up.

Typical minimum stay:

- Efficient checkup without colonoscopy or multiple specialists: 2-3 days.
- Checkup with colonoscopy/sedation or several specialists: 3-5 days.
- Checkup with abnormal findings requiring biopsy/advanced imaging: open-ended; build contingency days.

## Screening Detail Rules

Use these as decision prompts, not automatic package inclusions:

- Blood pressure: screen adults 18+; if high, recommend confirmation outside clinic before diagnosis/treatment unless urgent.
- Diabetes: USPSTF recommends screening adults 35-70 with overweight or obesity; consider local guidelines and risk factors.
- Colorectal cancer: ACS recommends average-risk screening starting at age 45; positive stool tests require timely colonoscopy.
- Breast cancer: ACS recommends optional annual mammography age 40-44, annual age 45-54, then every 2 years or yearly from 55+ while health/life expectancy support screening.
- Cervical cancer: ACS recommends screening from age 25 to at least 65 for people with a cervix, with HPV-based options and intervals depending on test type.
- Lung cancer: annual low-dose CT is for people meeting age and smoking-risk criteria; do not offer as routine screening to never-smokers.
- Prostate cancer: use shared decision-making for people with a prostate, based on age, risk, and life expectancy.
- Vaccines: review against current CDC/local adult immunization schedule and contraindications.

Low-value or caution items:

- Do not sell whole-body CT/MRI, broad tumor-marker panels, or excessive cardiac testing as routine preventive care without risk-based indication.
- Do not label incidental findings as diagnoses without clinician interpretation.
- Do not reassure away symptoms because a screening test is normal.

Urgent signs:

- Chest pain, stroke symptoms, severe shortness of breath, fainting, severe abdominal pain, uncontrolled bleeding, suicidal ideation, or any acute severe symptom. Route to urgent/emergency care.

Ask the medical check center:

- Which tests are guideline-based for this patient's age/risk, and which are optional add-ons?
- Who reviews all results with the patient, and in what language?
- How are critical findings handled before the final report?
- How are pending pathology/imaging reports delivered after travel?
- Who coordinates referrals in the patient's home country?
