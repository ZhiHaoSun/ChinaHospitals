# CAR-T Timeline Data

Use for CAR-T therapy, chimeric antigen receptor T-cell therapy, cellular immunotherapy, and related medical-travel or oncology pathway planning. CAR-T is high-risk specialist oncology care; never present it as a routine elective package.

## Trusted Sources

- National Cancer Institute, CAR-T overview: https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/research/car-t-cells
- National Cancer Institute, T-cell transfer therapy: https://www.cancer.gov/about-cancer/treatment/types/immunotherapy/t-cell-transfer-therapy
- UCLH NHS, CAR-T patient pathway: https://www.uclh.nhs.uk/patients-and-visitors/patient-information-pages/car-t-cell-therapy
- Memorial Sloan Kettering, adult CAR-T patient guide: https://www.mskcc.org/cancer-care/patient-education/car-cell-therapy-guide-adult-patients-caregivers
- EBMT/EHA CAR-T Cell Handbook: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK584162/
- Product label, treating CAR-T center protocol, trial protocol, and hematology/oncology team instructions override all generic ranges.

## Required Intake Details

Collect or state assumptions for:

- Cancer type, disease status, prior lines of therapy, urgency, tumor burden.
- CAR-T product or trial, target antigen, autologous vs allogeneic if known.
- Treating center and pathway: inpatient, outpatient, hybrid, trial-specific.
- Eligibility workup status: organ function, infection status, CNS involvement, performance status, caregiver, insurance/authorization.
- Leukapheresis date, manufacturing slot, bridging therapy need, product arrival date.
- Local-stay rule: required distance from hospital, caregiver availability, emergency hotline, language support.
- Travel risks: medical visa, infection exposure, air travel after lymphodepletion/infusion, lodging near center.

## Baseline Timeline

| Timing | Usual activity | Required details | Planning notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Week -12 to -8 | Referral and records transfer | Pathology, imaging, prior treatment records, molecular/antigen data, performance status | Start early if international records, translation, insurance, or trial screening are involved. |
| Week -8 to -4 | Eligibility and baseline workup | Blood tests, organ function, infection screening, disease assessment, pregnancy/contraception when relevant, caregiver plan | Center decides eligibility; do not promise approval. |
| Week -6 to -3 | Leukapheresis/T-cell collection | Venous access/catheter, collection labs, consent | UCLH describes leukapheresis as about 6 hours including checks; NCI identifies collection as the first manufacturing step. |
| Week -6 to -1 | Manufacturing and bridging therapy | Product manufacturing status, disease control plan, local oncologist coordination | NCI says collection-to-infusion commonly takes about 3-5 weeks; UCLH describes at least 4-6 weeks in its pathway. |
| Day -7 to -1 | Lymphodepleting/conditioning chemotherapy | Regimen, line access, anti-infective plan, admission/outpatient schedule | MSK says lymphodepleting chemo is usually a few days before infusion; EBMT/EHA notes 5-7 days ahead in some protocols. |
| Day -1 to 0 | Fitness-to-infuse check | Vitals, infection symptoms, labs, product verification | Infusion may be delayed if unsafe. |
| Day 0 | CAR-T infusion | Product ID checks, premedication if ordered, infusion monitoring | UCLH describes infusion after thawing as about 10-20 minutes; Day 0 anchors post-infusion counting. |
| Day +1 to +14 | Highest-risk monitoring period | Fever, blood pressure, oxygen, neuro checks, blood counts, CRS/ICANS, infection, tumor lysis | UCLH describes at least 14 inpatient days; some centers do outpatient daily visits with rapid admission. |
| Day +15 to +28 | Close monitoring near center | 24-hour caregiver, daily or less frequent visits, emergency access | UCLH requires staying within 1 hour for a further 14 days after minimum inpatient stay; MSK requires nearby lodging if far from center. |
| Week +4 to +12 | Early recovery and response assessment | Imaging/bone marrow/labs per cancer type, cytopenias, infections, neurologic recovery | Frequency decreases only when center allows. |
| Month +3 to +12 | Continued oncology follow-up | Disease response, immune recovery, infection prophylaxis, immunoglobulin needs | Vaccination and prophylaxis plans are specialist-directed. |
| Year +1 onward | Long-term follow-up | Relapse surveillance, late toxicities, secondary malignancy monitoring | Some programs follow patients long term; UCLH service page notes CNS contact for up to 15 years. |

Typical minimum stay:

- From leukapheresis through product manufacturing and infusion: often 6-10+ weeks total, depending on manufacturing and bridging.
- Near CAR-T center after infusion: at least through Day +28 in many pathways; never shorten without written center approval.
- International patient planning: assume multiple phases or a long continuous stay unless the center coordinates local bridging and pre-infusion care.

Cannot miss:

- Eligibility and infection screening.
- Confirmed product availability before lymphodepletion when required by protocol.
- Day 0 infusion monitoring.
- First 28 days of close monitoring with caregiver and rapid emergency access.
- Long-term oncology follow-up.

Urgent signs:

- Fever, chills, confusion, trouble speaking/writing, seizure, severe headache, dizziness/fainting, breathing trouble, fast heartbeat, low blood pressure symptoms, bleeding, severe nausea/vomiting/diarrhea, or any sudden neurologic change.

Ask the CAR-T center:

- Is the pathway inpatient, outpatient, or hybrid, and what triggers admission?
- What exact local-stay radius and caregiver rules apply through Day +28?
- How long is manufacturing expected for this product, and what happens if manufacturing fails or is delayed?
- What bridging therapy is planned and where can it be given?
- What emergency number should be used after hours, and which hospital should receive the patient in an emergency?
- What restrictions apply to driving, flying, crowds, vaccines, diet, central line care, and work?
- What long-term follow-up schedule is required by product label, trial, or center policy?

Output caution:

- Do not estimate candidacy, response probability, or survival unless the user provides clinician-approved materials and asks for a summary.
- Do not convert CAR-T into a "package price itinerary" without emphasizing clinical eligibility and toxicity monitoring.
