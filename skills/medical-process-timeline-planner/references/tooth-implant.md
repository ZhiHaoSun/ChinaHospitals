# Tooth Implant Timeline Data

Use for single-tooth dental implants, multi-tooth implants, implant-supported dentures, sinus lift, bone graft, abutment, crown, and long-term implant maintenance planning.

## Required Intake Details

Collect or state assumptions for:

- Tooth/teeth involved, single vs multiple vs full arch.
- Current tooth status: already missing, extraction needed, active infection, periodontal disease.
- Bone status: CBCT available, bone graft needed, sinus lift needed, upper posterior jaw involvement.
- Restoration plan: crown, bridge, removable overdenture, fixed full-arch restoration, immediate temporary tooth, immediate loading.
- Medical risks: diabetes control, smoking/vaping, bisphosphonates/anti-resorptives, immunosuppression, anticoagulants, radiation history, autoimmune disease, allergies.
- Provider roles: who places implant, who restores crown/bridge/denture, who handles maintenance.
- Travel plan: number of trips acceptable, availability for 1-week wound check, ability to return months later.

## Baseline Single-Tooth Timeline

| Timing | Usual activity | Required details | Planning notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Day -60 to -7 | Consultation and diagnosis | Dental exam, periodontal status, medical history, panoramic X-ray and/or CBCT, bite/restoration plan | ACP emphasizes careful diagnosis and treatment planning; identify whether specialist referral is needed. |
| Day -30 to -1 | Pre-surgical planning | Surgical guide if needed, anesthesia/sedation plan, consent, antibiotics/medicines if prescribed | Confirm if extraction and implant can be same day or staged. |
| Day -90 to -1 | Extraction, infection control, bone graft if needed | Tooth removal, graft material, healing interval | Grafting can add months; timeline depends on bone volume and infection. |
| Day 0 | Implant placement | Incision, jawbone preparation, implant placement, sutures, temporary tooth/loading decision | Usually outpatient. AAOMS describes implant fusing with jawbone through osseointegration. |
| Day +1 to +3 | Early recovery | Bleeding, swelling, pain control, soft foods, oral hygiene | Many patients resume routine activities in 2-3 days; avoid strenuous exercise for at least 48 hours unless clinician says otherwise. |
| Day +7 to +14 | Wound check/suture removal | Healing, infection signs, suture type, hygiene | Guy's and St Thomas' notes stitches are removed about a week later when used. |
| Month +3 to +9 | Osseointegration | Implant stability, X-rays if needed, gum health | Cleveland Clinic describes osseointegration as 3-9 months; NHS notes waiting at least 3 months before replacement teeth. |
| Month +3 to +10 | Abutment and final restoration | Abutment, impressions/scans, crown/bridge/denture try-in, bite check | May require several appointments; buried implants may need a small second-stage procedure. |
| Long term | Maintenance | Professional cleaning, probing/radiographs as indicated, home care | ACP emphasizes regular professional checkups and cleanings; full-arch maintenance can be risk-based. |

Typical minimum stay:

- Consult plus uncomplicated implant placement: 5-10 on-site days to include early wound review.
- Extraction + immediate implant: 7-14 days if early healing needs local review.
- Final crown/restoration: usually later trip after osseointegration unless immediate loading is clinically appropriate.

## Added Timeline For Bone Graft Or Sinus Lift

| Timing | Usual activity | Planning notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Day -180 to -90 | Bone graft or sinus augmentation if needed | AAP describes sinus augmentation bone development as often 4-12 months before implants, depending on individual needs. |
| Month +4 to +12 after graft | Imaging and implant readiness review | Confirm bone quantity/quality and sinus/graft healing before implant. |
| Day 0 after readiness | Implant placement | Some cases permit implant at same time as sinus augmentation; confirm case-specific plan. |

Typical minimum stay:

- Sinus lift or major graft: 7-14 days for procedure and early review, then a later implant trip after healing.
- Full treatment: commonly 6-12+ months from planning to final restoration if grafting is needed.

## Maintenance Details

Include long-term instructions in every implant timeline:

- Brush twice daily and clean around implants as instructed.
- Use floss, interdental cleaners, or water irrigator if recommended and safe for the restoration.
- Schedule professional maintenance based on risk profile.
- Watch for peri-implant inflammation: redness, swelling, bleeding, pus, deepening pockets, pain, mobility, or bite changes.
- Full-arch fixed restorations may need specialized access/cleaning; do not remove unless the dentist indicates.

Urgent signs:

- Uncontrolled bleeding, fever, spreading swelling, pus, severe pain not improving, new numbness, implant mobility, or breathing/swallowing difficulty.

Ask the dental team:

- Is bone grafting/sinus lift needed, and how many months before implant placement?
- Is immediate provisionalization/loading allowed, and can the temporary tooth be used for chewing?
- Which visits must be in person and which can happen locally?
- Who owns complications after travel: surgeon, restorative dentist, or local dentist?
- What are the maintenance interval and expected future restoration replacement needs?
