# Eye Surgery Timeline Data

Use for cataract surgery, LASIK, and other common planned refractive eye surgery timelines. Always distinguish the procedure type before creating a calendar.

## Trusted Sources

- National Eye Institute, cataract surgery: https://www.nei.nih.gov/eye-health-information/eye-conditions-and-diseases/cataracts/cataract-surgery
- FDA, LASIK before/during/after expectations: https://www.fda.gov/MedicalDevices/ProductsandMedicalProcedures/SurgeryandLifeSupport/LASIK/ucm061270.htm
- FDA, LASIK surgery checklist: https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/lasik/lasik-surgery-checklist
- American Academy of Ophthalmology Preferred Practice Pattern sources when current and accessible.
- Treating ophthalmologist or surgical center discharge protocol overrides generic ranges.

## Required Intake Details

Collect or state assumptions for:

- Procedure: cataract, LASIK, PRK, SMILE, ICL, glaucoma/retina-related surgery, or other.
- One eye or both eyes; if both, same-day bilateral vs staged.
- Current eye diseases: glaucoma, retina disease, diabetic eye disease, dry eye, corneal disease, prior eye surgery.
- Visual goal: monofocal, multifocal, toric, monovision, spectacle independence, occupational needs.
- Medicines: blood thinners, alpha blockers such as tamsulosin, steroids, diabetes medicines, glaucoma drops, allergy to drops.
- Contact lens type and last date worn for LASIK/refractive evaluation.
- Travel constraints: flight date, escort availability, ability to attend early follow-up, local ophthalmologist for later checks.

## Cataract Baseline Timeline

Use for uncomplicated cataract surgery unless the surgeon says otherwise.

| Timing | Usual activity | Required details | Planning notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Day -30 to -7 | Ophthalmology consultation | Visual acuity, slit-lamp exam, retina/optic nerve status, cataract severity, daily-function limitation, medical/medicine review | Add extra clearance time for diabetes, glaucoma, macular degeneration, anticoagulation questions, or complex cataract. |
| Around Day -7 | Biometry and lens planning | Eye size/shape measurements, corneal measurements, IOL choice, astigmatism plan | NEI describes tests about a week before surgery to choose the artificial lens. |
| Day -7 to -1 | Pre-op instructions | Drops if prescribed, eyelid hygiene if instructed, fasting/anesthesia plan, consent, escort | Confirm whether the patient must stop any medicine; do not advise stopping without clinician approval. |
| Day 0 | Cataract surgery | Eye marked, anesthesia/drops, lens removal and IOL placement, recovery-area eye check | Usually outpatient; NEI describes the surgery as about 1 hour and almost painless for many patients. |
| Day +1 to +2 | Early post-op review | Vision, pain/redness, eye pressure if indicated, wound/lens position, drop schedule | Use 24-48 hours as a common early review window; high-risk or complicated cases need surgeon-directed timing. |
| Week +1 | Healing check | Inflammation, infection signs, drop adherence, activity restrictions | Review avoiding eye rubbing, bending/heavy lifting if instructed, and protective shield/glasses. |
| Week +4 to +8 | Refraction and final healing check | Vision stability, glasses prescription, retina status if needed | NEI notes most people are completely healed about 8 weeks after surgery. |
| About 1 month after first eye | Second-eye surgery if planned | Confirm first-eye healing and updated refractive target | NEI notes both eyes are usually done separately, often about a month apart. |

Typical minimum stay:

- One uncomplicated cataract eye: 3-7 on-site days if Day +1/+2 review is completed before departure.
- Both eyes staged locally: often 4-6 weeks or two trips, depending on surgical interval.
- Complex cataract, diabetes/retina/glaucoma, or travel without local follow-up: plan longer and require surgeon approval before flying.

Cannot miss:

- Biometry/IOL planning before surgery.
- Early post-op review if surgeon protocol, complication, high-risk eye, pain, or vision concern.
- Full course of prescribed drops and activity limits.

Urgent signs:

- Vision loss, severe or worsening pain, very red eye, flashes/floaters, discharge, trauma, or rapidly worsening symptoms.

Ask the eye center:

- Exact follow-up schedule and whether Day +1 review is mandatory.
- When the patient can fly, exercise, swim, wear makeup, drive, and restart medicines.
- Whether later checks can be done by a local ophthalmologist after travel.
- What emergency number to call after hours.

## LASIK/Refractive Baseline Timeline

Use for LASIK unless the user says PRK/SMILE/ICL; adjust if the procedure has slower surface healing or intraocular surgery.

| Timing | Usual activity | Required details | Planning notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Day -30 to -14 | Stop contact lenses before baseline evaluation | Lens type and last-worn date | FDA examples: soft lenses 2 weeks, toric soft or RGP 3 weeks, hard lenses 4 weeks before initial evaluation. |
| Day -14 to -1 | Baseline refractive evaluation | Refraction stability, corneal thickness/topography, pupil size, dry eye, occupation, expectations | FDA checklist highlights candidacy, risks, alternatives, device approval, and informed consent. |
| Day -1 | Pre-op hygiene and logistics | No makeup/lotion/perfume if instructed, transport home, first follow-up transport | FDA advises arranging transport for surgery and first follow-up. |
| Day 0 | LASIK/refractive procedure | Procedure type, eye shield, drop regimen, no eye rubbing | Vision may be hazy/blurry; discomfort, tearing, light sensitivity can occur early. |
| Day +1 to +2 | First post-op visit | Shield removal if used, vision test, corneal/flap check, drop review | FDA advises seeing the doctor within 24-48 hours after surgery. |
| Day +3 to +14 | Early recovery | Dry eye, light sensitivity, return to work, makeup/water restrictions | FDA notes symptoms should improve in first few days; avoid swimming/hot tubs for 1-2 months or per doctor. |
| Month +1 to +6 | Regular follow-up | Vision stability, glare/halos, night driving, dry eye | FDA describes regular intervals for at least 6 months and possible 3-6 months for stabilization. |

Typical minimum stay:

- Uncomplicated LASIK: 3-5 on-site days to include first follow-up.
- PRK or surface ablation: often longer early healing and more frequent early checks; confirm with surgeon.
- Enhancement planning: do not schedule until measurements are stable per surgeon; FDA notes enhancement should wait for stable measurements.

Cannot miss:

- Contact lens discontinuation before measurements.
- First 24-48 hour post-op review.
- No eye rubbing and adherence to drops/artificial tears.

Urgent signs:

- Severe pain, worsening vision, new unusual symptoms, increasing redness, or symptoms that worsen instead of improving.

Ask the eye center:

- Which FDA-approved device/procedure is used, expected outcome range, and alternatives.
- Return-to-work, flying, swimming, makeup, sports, and night-driving timeline.
- Exact follow-up schedule for local and overseas patients.
